# Workflow Verification And Closeout Spec

This stable workflow spec fixture anchors the expected verification and closeout surface for a MyLittleHarness operating root.

Repo-visible project files remain authoritative. This fixture is repair-created only when absent, and it cannot approve repair, closeout, archive, commit, rollback, lifecycle decisions, or future mutations.

This packaged stable-spec template is an intentionally minimal bootstrap stub: a repaired live root must treat it as review-required until human-maintained stable specs expand or replace it.

Verified phase work stops at repo-visible state/evidence unless the active plan explicitly opts into `auto_continue` and records stop-condition coverage. Closeout preparation remains an explicit boundary, not an automatic side effect of passing checks. `writeback --active-phase <next-phase> --phase-status pending` is the explicit same-plan phase-advance rail; `writeback --phase-status complete` is a ready-for-closeout boundary only. A same-request `docs_decision = uncertain` plus `phase_status = complete` may replace mismatched prior closeout facts with current plan identity and provisional docs posture, but it still cannot carry stale closeout facts or approve archive, roadmap done-status, source-incubation archive, or next-slice opening. Terminal active-plan closeout is first-class: `writeback --archive-active-plan --phase-status blocked|deferred|abandoned|skipped` may archive an honestly unsuccessful plan only with reviewed blocker evidence, verification, carry-forward or next-state text, and a non-done roadmap status when a roadmap item is updated; it must not convert blocked work to complete or imply source, staging, commit, push, repair, release, or next-plan approval. During archive close, stale `MLH Phase Writeback` tails that reference the default active-plan route are retired so project-state no longer points at the deleted plan. Archive relationship retargeting also includes stable workflow specs whose implementation metadata points at the default active-plan route, so accepted specs do not retain `implemented_by: project/implementation-plan.md` after that route is archived. Existing `project/verification/*.md` artifacts are reported as pre-archive lifecycle snapshots after archive route writes; final post-archive evidence must come from route-write evidence, `check`, or explicitly regenerated verification. When project-state already has complete closeout authority for the active plan, archive close may carry those matching facts, retarget the closeout identity to the archived plan, and write the synchronized archived-plan copy in the same transaction even when the operator did not resupply every closeout field. Writeback refusal output and `suggest --intent "phase closeout handoff"` surface the composed safe sequence for splitting phase evidence handoff from archive closeout replacement: first review `writeback --phase-status complete --docs-decision uncertain`, then review `writeback --archive-active-plan` with complete closeout facts and no explicit `--active-phase` or `--last-archived-plan`. `suggest --intent "blocked active plan closeout"` surfaces the terminal archive route for blocked, deferred, abandoned, or skipped active plans so operators do not have to choose between fake completion and plan cancellation. After a plan is already archived, `writeback --roadmap-item <id> --archived-plan <project/archive/plans/...>` may refresh same-request closeout, roadmap, and source-incubation facts for that archived identity without reopening lifecycle state. Lifecycle-field writeback that would also trigger project-state auto-compaction refuses before writing unless the operator explicitly supplies `--allow-auto-compaction` after review or runs `writeback --compact-only` as a separate maintenance step.

Read-only closeout helpers may use project-state closeout writeback facts for candidate fields and Git trailer suggestions only when the recorded plan identity matches the current active or archived plan; mismatched closeout identity is stale evidence and must be ignored in favor of current active-plan or same-request transition facts. When `plan_status = none`, `active_plan` is empty, `last_archived_plan` matches the recorded archived identity, and the matching roadmap item is already `done`, carry-forward text that only says to archive the active plan and mark the roadmap item done is satisfied historical context, not a current closeout candidate, Git trailer suggestion, or next action.

Read-only check and grain diagnostics may flag missing, generic, unresolved, or toolchain-mismatched verification gates, adjacent regression-test ownership gaps, raw-log-heavy plans, done roadmap items without archived evidence, done roadmap items whose `docs_decision` remains `uncertain` while archived evidence is missing, and weak archived-plan closeout samples. These findings are closeout prompts and calibration evidence only; they cannot mark work verified, write closeout state, archive a plan, or change roadmap status.

`reconcile` and `check --focus agents` are read-only verification aids. They may report route, source, and evidence fingerprints, classify synced, partially_verified, stale, drift_detected, and unassessed posture, and surface stale claims, stale approval packets, missing run evidence, no-progress residue, abandoned worktree references, and worker-space residue. They assess the manifest default active-plan route only when project-state marks a plan active or names `active_plan`; an inactive empty active-plan pointer is lazy scope, not a missing-route warning. Their human-gated proposals cannot release claims, delete worker outputs, rewrite authority, archive a plan, stage, commit, or approve lifecycle movement.
